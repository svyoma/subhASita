What these insights reveal
  • Shared pādas — identical quarter-verses across texts point to verses from a common oral/written source, conscious quotation, or the same verse being anthologised multiple times.
  • TF-IDF similarity — verses scoring high on character n-gram overlap often share the same topic, meter, or even the same poet's style.
  • Word frequency — words like rāja, kavi, dharma reflect the dominant registers of the subhāṣita genre.
  • Pāda count — 2-pāda entries are half-verses (often anuṣṭubh hemistichs), while 4-pāda entries are complete ślokas in more elaborate meters.
